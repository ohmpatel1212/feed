Six visual identities for the Feeds · Posts · Chat/Tune shell.

Same 3-column structure, same content, same segmented toggle. Only the typography, palette, density, and tone change. Pick the mood that feels like Ripple.

01 / Editorial Garden

Editorial Garden

The current direction, polished. Warm cream paper, sage and aurora greens, Instrument Serif for titles, Inter for body. Calm, magazine-y, optimistic. Feels like sitting down with a good newsletter on a Sunday morning.

02 / Linen & Ink

Linen & Ink

Cooler, more Notion-adjacent. Off-white background, deep navy ink instead of black, muted teal accent, Fraunces for the few moments of personality. Square corners, sharp hairlines. Reads as serious, well-organized, professional. Less warmth, more precision.

03 / Apothecary

Apothecary

Botanical, romantic, the most "designed." Deep cream paper, forest green and sepia, italic serifs everywhere, dotted dividers, ornamental flourishes (✻). Feels like a hand-bound notebook. Highest character; risks feeling precious or hard to maintain.

italic serifs forest + sepia ornamental most character

04 / Concrete

Concrete

Modernist, brutalist-lite, opinionated. Stark off-white, thick black rules, JetBrains Mono for labels and chrome, a single hot ember accent. Square corners, all-caps, no decoration. Reads as a workshop tool, not a magazine. Feels powerful and a little intimidating.

mono-heavy thick rules single hot accent workshop tool

05 / Sketchbook

Sketchbook

Personal, handwritten, lo-fi. Beige notebook paper with faint rules, dashed borders, Caveat for headers and labels, Fraunces for the body. The chat user-bubble becomes handwriting. Charming and approachable; carries the implicit promise that the product is a small, personal thing rather than enterprise.

notebook paper handwritten dashed borders may feel toy-ish

06 / Atelier Minimal

Atelier Minimal

Whitespace, hairlines, ultra-restrained. Pure white, Inter throughout in light weight (300), generous spacing, almost no color — a single thin aurora line marking the active state. Borrows from Aesop / Glossier / Apple marketing pages. Most premium-feeling; can read as cold if not balanced with content density.
