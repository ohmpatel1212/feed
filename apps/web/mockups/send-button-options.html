Send button: keep it exotic, fix the read

Six takes. A to D keep the same liquid metal shader the button uses today (the real library, live below) and only change its colors and the arrow treatment. E and F trade the shader for other kinds of exotic. Each card shows the button in context next to the input pill, plus a 2x close-up.

The problem: the amber midtones sit close to the arrow's white at this size, and the 2px stroke dissolves into the swirl.

Option A

Same metal, arrow on a plate

Describe your ideal feed…

Minimal change: exact same shader, but a frosted dark disc sits under a thicker arrow. The metal keeps swirling around the edges; the centre is always readable.

Option B

Chrome, dark arrow

Describe your ideal feed…

The liquid-logo look: bright silver chrome (the classic Paper Shaders demo aesthetic) flips the contrast problem, a near-black arrow on bright metal is always visible.

Option C

Aurora metal (brand greens)

Describe your ideal feed…

On brand: deep ink-green base with the aurora green as the metal tint, ties the button to the live badge and the pipeline dot. Cream arrow with a dark halo for the read.

Option D

Molten gold, ink arrow

Describe your ideal feed…

Same warmth, more light: keeps today's amber family but pushes the tint much brighter so the metal reads gold instead of murky bronze, and the arrow goes dark.

Option E

Holographic foil (CSS only)

Describe your ideal feed…

No WebGL: a conic gradient through the palette with a slow hue breathe, holographic foil feel. Cheaper than the shader and never fails to load, but less alive up close.

Option F

Ink core, glow ring

Describe your ideal feed…

Apple Intelligence energy: solid ink centre for a perfectly crisp arrow, with a rotating aurora-to-amber gradient ring and soft outer glow carrying the exotic. Maximum legibility.
