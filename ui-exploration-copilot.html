Copilot variants · how to surface tuning

Six ways to put the tune next to the chat next to the feed.

The chat copilot stays the lead instrument. What changes is where the model of your taste lives — whether it’s inline in the assistant’s words, a quiet recipe card in the corner, a rail on the side, a sheet you summon, or woven directly into the post stream. Each cell labels the tradeoff it’s making.

01 / Tuning lives in the conversation

Interactive Transcript

Tradeoff: Tuning is contextual. Every chip the assistant proposes sits inside the message that created it — click to keep, click again to drop. Nothing else competes for attention. But scrolling back to find “where did I mute ‘collapse’ again?” becomes archaeology.

Ambient Recipe

Reading-First

Tradeoff: Optimized for what users actually came to do — read. The chat collapses to a single suggestion bar with a Spotlight-style invocation (⌘K). Tuning lives in a slim right rail. Best for power readers; first-timers may not realize there’s a copilot at all.

04 / Modes, with a sticky receipt

Tabbed Workspace

Tradeoff: Clear separation of concerns — Chat, Tune, both feed-backed by a live preview pane. A sticky summary strip means you never lose sight of what the feed currently is. The cost: switching tabs creates micro-context-loss; some users will live in just one tab and forget the other exists.

05 / Chat and feed, side by side

Equal Split + Tune Sheet

Tradeoff: 50/50 layout lets you watch the feed change as you chat. Tuning lives behind a single pill that opens a focused sheet — one job at a time. Best for fast iteration. Cost: the modal is a context shift, and per-post feedback can quietly do most of the work, making the sheet feel optional.

06 / Chat woven into the stream

Conversational Feed

Tradeoff: The whole product is a single column. The recipe is an editable masthead at top, posts are the body, the assistant occasionally interjects with a card noticing patterns (“you scrolled past three collapse posts — mute?”). Most ambient and magazine-like. Cost: the assistant has to be tasteful about when to speak, or it gets annoying fast.
